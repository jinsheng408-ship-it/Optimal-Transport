Computational Optimal Transport & Sinkhorn Algorithm

Optimal transport studies how to transform one probability distribution into another in a way that minimises a transportation cost. In its discrete form, this involves finding a coupling matrix \(P\) that moves mass from the source distribution \(a\) to the target distribution \(b\) at minimal cost. The classical Kantorovich formulation solves a linear programme over the admissible set \[U(a,b) = \bigl\{P \ge 0 : P\mathbf{1} = a,\; P^{\!\top}\mathbf{1} = b\bigr\}.\]

Entropic regularisation adds a Shannon entropy penalty \(-\varepsilon H(P)\), smoothing the solution and dramatically speeding up computation. The objective becomes \[\min_{P\in U(a,b)} \langle C,P\rangle - \varepsilon H(P).\] Strong convexity of the KL divergence guarantees a unique minimiser for every \(\varepsilon > 0\), and the plan becomes more diffuse as \(\varepsilon\) grows.

The Sinkhorn algorithm solves this problem efficiently by alternately normalising rows and columns of the kernel matrix \(K = e^{-C/\varepsilon}\), producing scaling vectors \(u\) and \(v\) such that \(P = \operatorname{diag}(u)\,K\,\operatorname{diag}(v)\) satisfies the marginal constraints.
